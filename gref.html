Griffith – The White Hawk
Full Name: Griffith
Aliases: The White Hawk, Leader of the Band of the Hawk, Femto (after Eclipse)
Weapon: Sword, Archery, Tactical Genius
Companion: Band of the Hawk members, Guts (former), Princess Charlotte (political ally)
Symbol: Hawk of Destiny

Personality
Griffith is charismatic, intelligent, and extraordinarily ambitious. He inspires unwavering loyalty from his followers with his charm, beauty, and visionary leadership. Calm and composed on the surface, he is strategic and manipulative when needed, willing to make morally questionable choices to achieve his dream. Beneath his elegance lies a mix of pride, obsession, and vulnerability, particularly when facing setbacks to his ambitions.

Background
Griffith formed the Band of the Hawk and quickly rose to fame as a brilliant mercenary leader. His ultimate goal has always been to create his own kingdom and transcend human limitations. He befriends Guts, who becomes his right-hand man, and they share a bond of mutual respect and ambition. Griffith’s imprisonment and torture after a failed political scheme leave him physically broken, leading to his fateful choice during the Eclipse. Sacrificing his followers, he transforms into Femto, a member of the God Hand, gaining supernatural power but losing his humanity.

Abilities & Strengths
- Tactical Genius – exceptional military strategist, able to turn any battle to his favor.
- Charisma – inspires absolute loyalty and devotion from allies.
- Combat Skill – skilled swordsman and marksman, though more known for leadership.
- Femto Powers – after the Eclipse, possesses supernatural abilities including manipulation of space, flight, and immense destructive power.
- Vision & Ambition – unwavering focus on his dream, willing to endure suffering and make sacrifices to achieve it.

Relationships
- Guts – Once his closest friend and ally, their bond is fractured by Griffith’s betrayal during the Eclipse.
- Casca – Former Band of the Hawk member; Griffith’s actions during the Eclipse traumatize her.
- Band of the Hawk – Followers who adored him; most are sacrificed in the Eclipse, solidifying his role as a tragic villain.
- Princess Charlotte – Political ally whose alliance aids Griffith’s rise in Midland.
- God Hand – Becomes Femto, joining these demonic beings after the Eclipse, marking him as a supernatural force.

Themes
Griffith represents ambition, destiny, and the moral cost of pursuing one’s dream. His story explores loyalty, sacrifice, obsession, and the thin line between heroism and villainy. Griffith embodies the allure and danger of charisma and ambition, showing how one person can inspire greatness or commit horror.
